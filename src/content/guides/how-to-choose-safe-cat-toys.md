## Start with your cat's play style

Some cats prefer chasing, some like batting, and others enjoy chewing or carrying toys. The safest toy is often the one that fits how your cat already likes to play.

## Look for simple construction

Toys with fewer detachable parts are easier to monitor. Long strings, tiny bells, or weakly attached feathers may need closer supervision.

## Rotate instead of overbuying

Cats often respond better to a smaller set of toys that changes every few days. Rotation keeps play fresh and reduces floor clutter.

## Keep safety checks easy

Inspect toys often for torn seams, exposed stuffing, sharp edges, or loose parts. Retire damaged toys early instead of waiting for full failure.
