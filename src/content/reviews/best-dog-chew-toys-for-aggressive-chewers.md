## Quick verdict

If your dog destroys soft plush toys in minutes, a firmer chew toy category is usually a better starting point. The best options balance durability, safe sizing, and enough texture to stay interesting.

## What matters most

### 1. Correct size

Choose a size that matches your dog's mouth and chewing strength. A toy that is too small can become a safety issue, while one that is too large may never get used.

### 2. Surface design

Ridged or grooved textures can help keep a dog engaged for longer sessions. Smooth toys may work for some pets, but many strong chewers prefer a toy with more bite feedback.

### 3. Realistic expectations

Even tough chew toys wear down over time. Owners should inspect toys often and replace them if large pieces start breaking off.

## Bottom line

For aggressive chewers, durable toys are less about finding something indestructible and more about finding the safest long-lasting option for your dog's size, energy level, and habits.
